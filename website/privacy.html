Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-04-20 · Version 1.0

CallSight AI ("CallSight", "we", "us") provides an AI-powered platform for recording, transcribing, and analyzing customer-facing conversations. This policy explains what personal data we process, why, and who we share it with.

1. Data we process

When you or your organization uses the platform, we may process the following categories of personal data on behalf of your organization (acting as a data processor under GDPR):

  • Audio recordings of voice calls, and their transcripts.
  • Email, SMS, WhatsApp, and chat messages exchanged with your contacts.
  • Contact identifiers: names, email addresses, phone numbers, employer.
  • AI-derived content: summaries, sentiment scores, action items, coaching notes, KB search results.
  • Operational metadata: call duration, timestamps, sentiment trends, agent identifiers.
  • Account data for your users: name, email, role, authentication identifiers (managed by Clerk).

2. Why we process it

  • To deliver the service you contracted us to provide.
  • To generate AI insights, coaching, follow-ups, and KB retrieval.
  • To improve our prompts and models, only when your tenant has explicitly opted in via features_enabled.data_use_for_improvement.
  • To investigate security incidents, prevent abuse, and comply with legal obligations.

3. Retention

Retention is configurable per tenant. By default audio is retained for 90 days and transcripts for 24 months; your contract may override these. Tenant administrators can request deletion of any contact's data via the DELETE /api/v1/tenants/me/contacts/{id} endpoint, which purges interactions, transcripts, KB pins, and vector entries.

4. Your GDPR & CCPA rights

If your data is processed on behalf of a CallSight customer, please contact that customer's administrator to exercise access, correction, deletion, portability, and objection rights. CallSight supports these rights technically via:

  • Access / portability: GET /api/v1/tenants/me/export returns a JSON bundle of every interaction, transcript, KB document, and integration record (minus OAuth tokens) owned by your tenant.
  • Erasure: the contact-scoped delete endpoint above.
  • Rectification: the transcript-correction and contact-update endpoints under /api/v1/contacts.

6. Call recording consent

Many jurisdictions require all parties to a call to be notified that the call is being recorded. Obtaining consent is the CallSight customer's responsibility. Our platform records the consent method per interaction via the consent_method, consent_timestamp, and consent_evidence_url fields; interactions submitted without a consent_method are rejected at ingest.
